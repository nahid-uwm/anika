CASE STUDY 01

Budget vs Actual Variance Pack + KPI Scorecard

TLDR: We built a standardized monthly FP&A reporting pack (in Excel and Power BI) that includes: a variance bridge (actual vs budget vs prior period) to clearly itemize drivers of change; a KPI scorecard with clear targets, color-coded RAG thresholds (green/amber/red), and trend charts for context; a structured commentary template (“What happened/Why/Next steps”) to translate variances into business insights; and embedded audit checks (balance and sum reconciliations, error flags) to catch broken links.

For example, JPMorgan’s Q4 2025 report showed net revenue of $46.8B (up 7%) driven by Markets revenue $8.2B (up 17%) – our pack would highlight such drivers visually. The outcome is a repeatable report that saves analysts hours of manual work (freeing them “to interpret the results” rather than crunch numbers) and provides a clear story for management.

The Challenge: Opaque Monthly Reporting

Many US FP&A teams close the books on Day 1–3 of each month, only to spend hours chasing numbers to explain the results. As one FP&A expert notes, “variance analysis is one of the most time-consuming parts of month-end reporting,” often requiring “countless hours” to compare actuals against budgets and craft commentary. In our case, the monthly pack existed but lacked clarity: spreadsheets contained data, but it wasn’t obvious why actuals deviated from plan. Teams often rebuild the same reports every month instead of reusing templates, further wasting time. We needed a solution that made drivers and trends transparent and repeatable.

Our Solution: A Standardized FP&A Pack

We designed a compact yet comprehensive reporting package using Excel for calculations/commentary and Power BI for visualization. Key components (based on FP&A best practices) include:

1. Variance Bridge (Actual vs Budget vs Prior Month)

We created a waterfall-style “bridge” chart/table that starts with either last period’s actuals or full-year budget and sequentially adds up each variance. This bridge explains the total change by line item (e.g. sales, COGS, expenses) or category. In FP&A terms, a “bridge” is simply “a way of explaining how two numbers compare to one another.” Bridges are highly versatile: CFO analysts use them for “Budget vs Actual” and “Prior Year vs Current Year” comparisons. In our pack, the bridge highlights both budget variances and period-over-period changes.

2. KPI Scorecard with Thresholds and Trends

We built a one-page KPI dashboard. Each key metric (e.g. revenue growth, EBITDA margin, cash burn) shows the current value, the target/budget, and simple sparkline or mini-chart for recent months. Importantly, we applied traffic-light thresholds:

  • ● Green: metric meets or exceeds target.
  • ● Amber: worse than target but better than last year (or within tolerance).
  • ● Red: worse than both target and prior year.

This RAG coloring aligns with industry practice and draws the eye to issues. We also include trend context: showing each KPI’s history so users can distinguish meaningful trends from one-off blips.

3. Commentary Template (“What / Why / Next Steps”)

Numbers alone aren’t enough, so we included a commentary text area with prompts. Following best practices, our template guides the analyst to write: What happened (in plain business terms), Why the variance occurred, and How it impacts the rest of the year. This mirrors expert advice that variance write-ups should tell a story.

4. Basic Audit Checks

To avoid errors and broken links, we built in simple validation formulas. We have balance checks (e.g. Assets = Liabilities + Equity) and summation checks. We flagged any mismatches with conditional formatting (turning flags red). In our pack we also color-code input vs calculation cells and locked formula cells.

5. Tools: Excel and Power BI

We leveraged Excel for the core variance calculations, commentary, and as the data source. Then we used Power BI to connect to the Excel data for interactive visuals (the KPI dashboard and dynamic bridges). This hybrid approach is common in FP&A: Excel remains king for flexible modeling, while Power BI helps turn numbers into visuals.

Outcome & Benefits

  • ✓ Clear insights: Key drivers of performance are explicit. The structured bridge shows exactly which lines over- or underperformed.
  • ✓ Faster reporting: By reusing templates and automation, analysts spend far less time “hunting through spreadsheets” and more time analyzing.
  • ✓ Consistency and accuracy: Every monthly report follows the same logic and layout. Audit checks catch errors early.
  • ✓ Actionable focus: With clear commentary prompts and visual cues, discussions during monthly reviews can be more strategic.

Key Points & Takeaways

  • Use a variance bridge (waterfall chart) to reconcile Actual vs Budget vs Prior figures.
  • Include a KPI scorecard with goals, RAG thresholds (Green/Amber/Red), and trend lines.
  • Structure narrative commentary as What / Why / How / Next Steps.
  • Embed audit checks (reconciliations, IFERROR flags) to catch broken links.
  • Leverage Excel for flexible modeling and Power BI for automated dashboards.
  • Standardize templates to avoid reinventing the wheel each month.
